Lesson 2 of 5

Social Life Vocabulary

Learn collocations related to social activities.

Hang Out

To spend time relaxing or socializing informally.

Example: "I hung out with my friends yesterday."

Eat Out

To eat at a restaurant rather than at home.

Example: "We ate out at a nice Italian place."

See a Movie

To go to the cinema to watch a film.

Example: "They saw a movie last weekend."
Lesson 3 of 5

Grammar Deep Dive: Simple Past

Mastering affirmative, negative, and question forms.

Affirmative Statements

For regular verbs, add -ed to the base verb. Irregular verbs must be memorized.

Regular
  • work → worked
  • live → lived
Irregular
  • go → went
  • eat → ate

Negative Form

Use did not (didn't) + base verb.

Subject + didn't + base verb
  • I didn't want to go.
  • I didn't wanted to go.

Question Form

Use did before the subject.

(Wh-) + did + subject + base verb
  • Did you go out?
  • Where did they eat?

In My Experience

Make

Creating, deciding, or producing something.

  • make a mistake
  • make a friend

Get

A change in state, emotion, or situation.

Have

Experiencing an event or feeling.

  • have fun
  • have a party
  • have a problem
